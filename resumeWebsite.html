US Navy Experience

Enlisted in 2009 as a Master-At-Arms (Military Police). During my eight years I had two tours, one in Washington and another in Belgium. In Washington I was in a joint command with Marines providing strategic weapons security. In the beginning, I would stand watch in tours and inspect vehicles. As I strived to better myself, I qualified to become an alarm monitoring sentry and even further a supiviser. I was in charge of 70 personnel over 35 watch stations on a day to day basis. When we would do convoy movements I would be in charge of 35 personnel over 15 watchstations. Some of my daily responsiblities included: Invetory of the armory, guard mount to make sure all sailors are ready to stand post, conducted drills for mission readiness and trained sailors for mission critical qualifications.

I transferred to Mons, Belgium to the headquarters of NATO to provide law enforcement and security. During my time there, I was a patrolman as well as shift supiviser. The work environment was unique in that I was provided the opporunity to work with 28 other nations. My day to day operations included: traffic enforcement, physical security of restricted areas, escorts for VIPs to include: King of Jordan, King and Queen of Belgium and other national and foreign dignitaries. The base hosted many festivals that I would provide security for, to include: SHAPEfest which would have upwards to 15000 people attending, SACEUR change over, Make-A-Wish and Oktoberfest. I would work inconjunction with the local federal police in order to keep SHAPE safe.
